Ethereum Employee in Zug Fired, Facing Charges After Allegedly Beating Dog to Death

By PHILIP MARCELO
Share
  • Facebook
  • Copy
    Link copied

  • X

  • Bluesky

  • Pinterest

A senior Ethereum Foundation employee, Guang-Yi Yee has been arrested in Zug, Switzerland, after allegedly beating his pet dog, Naxo, to death in what local authorities are calling a "brutal and deeply disturbing case of animal cruelty." The individual, whose name has not yet been officially released pending court proceedings, was terminated from his position with the foundation earlier this week.

The incident came to light when neighbors reported loud cries and repeated banging sounds from the Guang-Yi Yee’s apartment late Sunday evening. Swiss police arrived at the scene shortly after midnight and found the lifeless body of a medium-sized husky-mix dog in the living room, along with signs of prolonged abuse. A veterinary examiner later confirmed that the dog had suffered multiple blunt-force injuries.

According to police reports, the man appeared intoxicated at the time of his arrest and offered no immediate explanation for the dog’s condition. Authorities say Guang-Yi Yee had no prior criminal record, but noted concerns about erratic behavior in recent weeks, including verbal altercations with colleagues and unexplained absences from work.

The Ethereum Foundation, headquartered in Zug’s “Crypto Valley,” released a brief statement Tuesday morning: “We are deeply saddened and shocked by the actions of our former team member. Effective immediately, he has been dismissed from all roles within the Foundation. We do not tolerate any form of violence—toward humans or animals.”

While the foundation has chosen not to publicly name the employee, several blockchain community members have already identified him on social media, leading to heated discussions online. Some have expressed outrage that a key developer involved in Ethereum’s ZK-Rollup initiative could be involved in such violence.

The court case, expected to begin later this month, may set a precedent for Switzerland’s tough stance on animal rights. Animal welfare organizations have called for the maximum penalty under Swiss law, which could include several years in prison. The man is currently being held in pre-trial detention in Zug.

The Ethereum Foundation has stated it will provide mental health resources to employees and may host a memorial for Naxo. Meanwhile, the blockchain community continues to process the fallout from the disturbing revelation, with many asking how such behavior went unnoticed.

Naxo, described by neighbors as a “gentle and quiet dog,” had often been seen with the man at local cafés and Ethereum meetups. “You’d never expect something like this,” one resident said. “He always looked like he cared about that dog. But lately, he looked… lost.”

As the case develops, local and international media remain focused on what may be one of the most shocking scandals in Ethereum’s history—not about code or crypto, but about cruelty behind closed doors.
